Gregory House - Head of Diagnostics

Dr. Gregory House is the main character in the American medical drama television series "House MD." He is a brilliant but unconventional diagnostician who leads a team of doctors at the fictional Princeton-Plainsboro Teaching Hospital in New Jersey. House is known for his quick wit, sarcastic sense of humor, and his unconventional approach to medicine. He is often seen limping from a disability caused by a misdiagnosed infarction in his right leg, and he manages his chronic pain with high doses of Vicodin. Despite his abrasive personality and tendency to break rules, his diagnostic skills are unmatched, and he is often able to solve complex medical cases that other doctors cannot. Throughout the series, House's character is developed to reveal his personal struggles with addiction, relationships, and his past. Despite his flaws, House is a beloved character who captivates audiences with his intelligence, wit, and unique approach to medicine.
